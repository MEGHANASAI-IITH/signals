    \textbf{Gate 2023 EE Q36} 
    The magnitude and phase plots of an LTI systems are shown in figure. Find the transfer function.\\
    \begin{figure}[h!]
        \centering
        \includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figs/gate.jpeg}
        \caption{Graphs}
        \label{fig:1ee36}
    \end{figure}
    
    \solution
    From the ~\figref{fig:1ee36}, we can infer that the magnitude of the transfer function does not change with $\omega$ and the phase is a function of $\omega$.
    \begin{align}
        \abs{{H}\brak{j\omega}}&= 20\log_{10}(\abs{{H}\brak{j\omega}}) \\
        \text{phase} &= \angle \frac{-\pi}{3}\omega
    \end{align}
    Substituting the values from~\figref{fig:1ee36}, magnitude of transfer function is:
    \begin{align}
        8 &= 20\log_{10}(\abs{{H}\brak{j\omega}})\\
        \abs{{H}\brak{j\omega}} &= 10^{0.4} = 2.511
    \end{align}
    Substituting the values from~\figref{fig:1ee36}, The direction of the transfer function is:
    \begin{align}
        \angle H\brak{j\omega} = e^{-j\frac{\pi}{3}\omega}
    \end{align}
    \begin{align}
        H\brak{j\omega} &= \abs{H\brak{j\omega}}\angle H\brak{j\omega}\\
        H\brak{j\omega} &= 2.511 e^{-j\frac{\pi}{3}\omega} \\
        &= 2.511 e^{-0.032s}
    \end{align}
